The Idea Behind This Portfolio

This is a KKP Wealth institutional managed portfolio designed for high-net-worth THB investors. It combines multiple mandate services and direct holdings across six distinct sleeves — each serving a clear purpose in building a diversified, globally balanced portfolio with enhanced inflation protection and capital preservation through significant gold and cash allocations.

Equity (4.74%)
Local Thai equity via VAYU1 and PTT, plus a small global stocks allocation for direct market exposure.
Mandate (37.52%)
KKP Balanced Standard (21.21% offshore) and KKP Moderate THB (16.31% onshore) — managed mandates providing diversified global exposure through equities, bonds, alternatives, and cash.
Alternative (25.38%)
Physical gold bullions (14.43%), private equity via Vista and KKR K-PRIME, global listed infrastructure, and fund of hedge funds — providing inflation protection, diversification, and illiquidity premium.
Satellite (5.15%)
Thematic equity bets across MedTech, Technology, Small Cap, India, and Europe — tactical growth at ~0.52% per position.
Cash (27.20%)
Fixed savings deposits providing capital preservation, liquidity, and stable income in the current rate environment.

The expanded portfolio achieves global diversification through KKP's mandate services, complemented by a substantial gold allocation for inflation protection, private equity for return enhancement, and fixed savings for capital preservation and liquidity.

Lost Decade Analysis

Portfolio resilience under prolonged adverse market environments

What is a Lost Decade?

A Lost Decade is a prolonged period (10+ years) when major equity indices deliver zero or negative real returns. Historical examples include Japan 1990–2010, the US 2000–2010, and the UK 1970s. Key characteristics: high valuations at entry, rising inflation, policy errors, geopolitical disruption, and currency instability. The expanded portfolio’s 14.43% gold and 27.20% cash allocations significantly improve resilience in stagflation and dollar crisis scenarios.

Scenario Analysis: Three Lost Decade Variants

Portfolio Sleeve Stagflation Secular Stagnation Dollar Crisis
Stagflation (1970s-style): High inflation + low growth. Winners: gold, commodities, infrastructure, EM debt. Losers: long-duration bonds, growth equity. Gold (14.43%) is a major beneficiary.
Secular Stagnation (Japan 1990s-style): Deflation + asset price decline. Winners: long bonds, cash, quality. Losers: equities, commodities. Cash (27.20%) provides significant buffer.
Dollar Crisis (de-dollarization): USD devaluation, capital flight. Winners: gold, non-USD assets, EM. Losers: US treasuries, USD cash. Gold (14.43%) is a strong hedge.
